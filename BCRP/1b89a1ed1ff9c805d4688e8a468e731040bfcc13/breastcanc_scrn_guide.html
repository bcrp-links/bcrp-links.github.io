For normal risk women, start screening mammograms at age 50 and repeat every 2 years.
Women aged 40-49 are not routinely screened unless known to be of high risk. You can use the Gail Model, a validated risk assessment tool, to determine whether a woman is high risk.
Women aged 20-49 should get a clinical breast exam every 1-3 years.
Women at moderate or high risk should be counseled on lifestyle modifications that may reduce the risk of breast cancer.
